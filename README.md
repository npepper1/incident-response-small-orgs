# Incident Response for Small Organizations

A practical incident response guide designed for small teams with limited staff, time, and tooling.

This repository focuses on **what actually works** when you do not have a SOC, dedicated IR team, or enterprise budget.

## Who this is for
- Small IT and security teams (2â€“10 people)
- Public-sector agencies without dedicated cyber units
- Small businesses and nonprofits
- School districts and local government
- Instructors teaching incident response fundamentals

## What this is
- A realistic incident response playbook
- Process-first, not tool-dependent
- Focused on decision-making, documentation, and communication
- Built for high-stress, low-resource environments

## What this is not
- A replacement for enterprise IR frameworks
- A forensic deep-dive manual
- A collection of tools without context
- A guide for unauthorized or unlawful activity

## Core principles
- Simplicity over complexity
- Clear authority and decision-making
- Preserve evidence before fixing systems
- Communicate early and carefully
- Document everything

## Repository structure
- **Pre-Incident Preparation**  
  Minimal steps that make incidents survivable
- **First 24 Hours Playbook**  
  What to do, in order, when something goes wrong
- **Roles for Small Teams**  
  How to assign responsibility when people wear multiple hats
- **Evidence Handling**  
  Lightweight, defensible preservation practices
- **Communication Templates**  
  Internal, leadership, and external messaging
- **Failure Modes**  
  Common mistakes that make incidents worse
- **Tabletop Scenarios**  
  Simple, teachable incident exercises

## How to use this repository
- As a reference during an incident
- As a tabletop or training resource
- As a starting point for building your own IR plan
- As instructional material for small-team environments
